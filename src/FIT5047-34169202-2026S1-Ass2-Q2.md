**Definition Recap: Chaining Algorithms and Clause Forms**

* **Definite Clause:** A disjunction of literals containing **exactly one positive literal**. In implication form, it is expressed as a conjunction of positive literals implying a single positive literal (e.g., $P_1 \wedge P_2 \dots \wedge P_n \Rightarrow Q$). They form the basis of efficient logical inference.
* **Forward Chaining (Data-Driven):** An inference method that begins with the known facts (observations) and iteratively applies rules to infer new facts until the goal is reached or no further facts can be deduced.
* **Backward Chaining (Goal-Driven):** An inference method that begins with the target goal and recursively works backward to find rules that satisfy the goal, breaking it down into sub-goals until it hits known facts.

</div>

### Q2.4: Discussion

#### (i) Does forward chaining stop early in this scenario? Why?
Yes, practically implemented Forward Chaining algorithms tailored for query-answering stop early. While pure data-driven FC continues until no new inferences can be made (which would mean processing $W$ via R6 and eventually halting when the agenda is fully empty), a query-directed FC algorithm implements a **goal-check trigger**. As demonstrated in Step 6, as soon as the target variable $S$ is inferred and added to the agenda, the algorithm halts and returns True. This avoids unnecessary processing of subsequent items on the agenda that have no bearing on the objective.

#### (ii) Could adding $\neg M$ or $A$ improve inference? Why or why not?

**Explanations for the Tutor (Dependency Graph Justification):**
In KRR, the relevance of any fact to a specific goal can be strictly evaluated using the **Dependency Graph** of the Knowledge Base. 

*   The goal node $S$ has incoming directed edges from $P, T,$ and $NS$.
*   Following the paths backward, the ultimate source nodes for the sub-graph connected to $S$ are exclusively $F$ and $E$.
*   The literal $M$ only has an outgoing edge to $NF$ (via R7). The literal $A$ (via R8) acts as a sink with no outgoing edges.
*   Neither $M$, $\neg M$, nor $A$ possess any directed paths that eventually reach $S$.

Therefore, injecting $\neg M$ or $A$ into the knowledge base would **not** improve, alter, or assist the inference of $S$. They exist in disjoint structural components of the dependency graph relative to the goal. Backward chaining will never traverse to them as sub-goals, and forward chaining will never use them to bridge a path toward $S$.
