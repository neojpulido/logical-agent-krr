**Definition Recap: FOL to CNF and Resolution Refutation**

* **Standardization of Variables:** The process of systematically renaming variables so that every quantifier binds a unique variable name. This prevents variable scope collisions when pulling quantifiers to the front of the formula (Prenex Normal Form).
* **Skolemization:** The elimination of existential quantifiers ($\exists$). If an existential variable is outside the scope of any universal quantifier, it is replaced by a **Skolem constant**. If it lies within the scope of universal quantifiers ($\forall$), it is replaced by a **Skolem function** that takes those universal variables as arguments, preserving its logical dependency.
* **Resolution Inference (Unification):** An inference rule where two clauses containing complementary literals (e.g., $P(x)$ and $\neg P(A)$) are resolved by finding a Most General Unifier (MGU) $\theta$ that makes the literals identical. The resolvent is the disjunction of the remaining literals subjected to $\theta$.
* **Refutation:** To prove a goal $G$, we negate it ($\neg G$), convert it to CNF, add it to the Knowledge Base (KB), and attempt to derive the empty clause ($\bot$). If $\bot$ cannot be derived and all paths are exhausted, the goal is unprovable.

### Q3(a): Step-by-Step Conversion to CNF

*Logical Convention Note: We interpret the dot notation (e.g., $\forall z.$) as scoping maximally to the right of the formula. Furthermore, free variables appearing in the knowledge base (e.g., $w, y$) are treated as implicitly universally quantified at the outermost level.*

**Clause 1: $\forall z. R(z) \Rightarrow \forall w. T(w)$**
1. Eliminate implication: $\forall z. (\neg R(z) \vee \forall w. T(w))$
2. Move quantifiers outward: $\forall z \forall w. (\neg R(z) \vee T(w))$
3. **CNF Result:** $\mathbf{\neg R(z) \vee T(w)}$ *(C1)*

**Clause 2: $\forall x. (\forall z. (P(x) \wedge R(z)) \Rightarrow (T(w) \rightarrow Q(y)))$**
1. Eliminate implications: $\forall x. (\neg \forall z. (P(x) \wedge R(z)) \vee (\neg T(w) \vee Q(y)))$
2. Push negation inward (De Morgan's): $\forall x. (\exists z. (\neg P(x) \vee \neg R(z)) \vee \neg T(w) \vee Q(y))$
3. Move quantifiers outward: $\forall x \exists z. (\neg P(x) \vee \neg R(z) \vee \neg T(w) \vee Q(y))$
4. **Skolemization:** $z$ is within the scope of $\forall x$, so it becomes a Skolem function $g(x)$.
5. **CNF Result:** $\mathbf{\neg P(x) \vee \neg R(g(x)) \vee \neg T(w) \vee Q(y)}$ *(C2)*

**Clause 3: $\neg(\forall x. (P(x) \Rightarrow T(w)) \wedge \forall x. (Q(y) \rightarrow P(x)))$**
1. Eliminate implications: $\neg(\forall x. (\neg P(x) \vee T(w)) \wedge \forall x. (\neg Q(y) \vee P(x)))$
2. Push negation inward (De Morgan's): $\neg \forall x. (\neg P(x) \vee T(w)) \vee \neg \forall x. (\neg Q(y) \vee P(x))$
3. Quantifier inversion: $\exists x. \neg(\neg P(x) \vee T(w)) \vee \exists x. \neg(\neg Q(y) \vee P(x))$
4. Push negation into clauses: $\exists x. (P(x) \wedge \neg T(w)) \vee \exists x. (Q(y) \wedge \neg P(x))$
5. **Standardization:** Rename bounded variables to avoid scope collision: $\exists x_1. (P(x_1) \wedge \neg T(w)) \vee \exists x_2. (Q(y) \wedge \neg P(x_2))$
6. **Skolemization:** Because $w$ and $y$ are implicitly universally quantified at the global scope, $x_1$ depends strictly on $w$, and $x_2$ depends on $y$. Replace $x_1 \mapsto f_1(w)$ and $x_2 \mapsto f_2(y)$.
7. Apply substitutions: $(P(f_1(w)) \wedge \neg T(w)) \vee (Q(y) \wedge \neg P(f_2(y)))$
8. Distribute $\vee$ over $\wedge$:
   * **CNF Result 3.1:** $\mathbf{P(f_1(w)) \vee Q(y)}$ *(C3.1)*
   * **CNF Result 3.2:** $\mathbf{P(f_1(w)) \vee \neg P(f_2(y))}$ *(C3.2)*
   * **CNF Result 3.3:** $\mathbf{\neg T(w) \vee Q(y)}$ *(C3.3)*
   * **CNF Result 3.4:** $\mathbf{\neg T(w) \vee \neg P(f_2(y))}$ *(C3.4)*

**Clause 4: $\forall x \exists z. (\neg R(z) \vee \neg Q(x))$**
1. **Skolemization:** $z$ is in the scope of $\forall x$, replacing $z \mapsto h(x)$.
2. **CNF Result:** $\mathbf{\neg R(h(x)) \vee \neg Q(x)}$ *(C4)*

**Clause 5: $\forall x. (U(x) \rightarrow (P(x) \vee \neg T(f(x))))$**
1. Eliminate implication: $\forall x. (\neg U(x) \vee P(x) \vee \neg T(f(x)))$
2. **CNF Result:** $\mathbf{\neg U(x) \vee P(x) \vee \neg T(f(x))}$ *(C5)*

**Clause 6: $\exists x. (Q(x) \wedge \neg U(x))$**
1. **Skolemization:** $x$ is bound existentially at the highest level (no enclosing $\forall$). Replace $x \mapsto d$ (Skolem constant).
2. Separate into distinct clauses:
   * **CNF Result 6.1:** $\mathbf{Q(d)}$ *(C6.1)*
   * **CNF Result 6.2:** $\mathbf{\neg U(d)}$ *(C6.2)*

**Clause 7: $\forall x \forall y.(\neg P(x) \vee Q(y) \vee S(x,y))$**
1. **CNF Result:** $\mathbf{\neg P(x) \vee Q(y) \vee S(x,y)}$ *(C7)*

**Goal (G): $\forall x. P(x)$**
1. Negate the goal: $\neg(\forall x. P(x)) \Rightarrow \exists x. \neg P(x)$
2. **Skolemization:** Replace $x \mapsto e$ (Skolem constant).
3. **CNF Result:** $\mathbf{\neg P(e)}$ *(CG)*

### Q3(b): Apply Resolution to Prove or Refute Goal (G)

To prove $G$, we must derive the empty clause ($\bot$) by combining the KB with the negated goal (CG). We implement a systematic resolution approach, prioritizing the **Pure Literal Strategy** to prune the search space.

**Agent Discovery: Pure Literal Domination**
Upon analyzing the predicate frequencies across all generated clauses, we observe profound structural disconnects:
* $R$ only appears as $\neg R$ (in C1, C2, C4). It is a pure negative literal.
* $U$ only appears as $\neg U$ (in C5, C6.2). It is a pure negative literal.
* $S$ only appears as positive $S$ (in C7). It is a pure positive literal.
By logically assigning False to $R$ and $U$, and True to $S$, we satisfy clauses C1, C2, C4, C5, C6.2, and C7 entirely. They cannot contribute to deriving $\bot$ and are mathematically eliminated from the resolution space.

</div>

**Resolution Trace:**

**Step 1:** Initial reduced Knowledge Base after primary pure literal elimination:
* C3.1: $P(f_1(w)) \vee Q(y)$
* C3.2: $P(f_1(w)) \vee \neg P(f_2(y))$
* C3.3: $\neg T(w) \vee Q(y)$
* C3.4: $\neg T(w) \vee \neg P(f_2(y))$
* C6.1: $Q(d)$
* CG: $\neg P(e)$

**Step 2:** Secondary Pure Literal Elimination:
* In the remaining active subset, $T$ only appears as $\neg T$ (in C3.3, C3.4). We assign $T = \text{False}$, eliminating C3.3 and C3.4.
* In the remaining active subset, $Q$ only appears as positive $Q$ (in C3.1, C6.1). We assign $Q = \text{True}$, eliminating C3.1 and C6.1.

**Step 3:** Final Evaluation:
* Remaining active clauses: **C3.2** ($P(f_1(w)) \vee \neg P(f_2(y))$) and **CG** ($\neg P(e)$).
* To derive $\bot$, we attempt to resolve C3.2 with CG. This requires unifying the positive literal $P(f_1(w))$ with the ground goal literal $P(e)$. 
* **Unification Failure:** The Skolem constant $e$ (representing an arbitrary entity that lacks property $P$) cannot unify with the Skolem function $f_1(w)$ (an entity structurally dependent on $w$). Even if unification were artificially forced, it would merely yield $\neg P(f_2(y))$, which has no complementary positive $P$ literal left in the KB to resolve against. 

**Conclusion:** The empty clause ($\bot$) **cannot be derived**. The negated goal is logically consistent (satisfiable) with the knowledge base. Therefore, the Goal $\forall x. P(x)$ is **not provable** from the provided clauses.

### Q3(c): Explanations for the Tutor

#### (i) Which clauses are redundant?
**Justification:** A clause is redundant in resolution if it contains a pure literal (rendering it satisfiable in isolation without aiding a proof chain) or if it is subsumed. 
* Clauses **1, 2, 4, 5, and 7** are entirely redundant because they contain pure literals ($\neg R$, $\neg U$, $S$). They sit in isolated partitions of the dependency graph and provide no complementary literal pathways back to the target predicate $P$.
* Clause **7** is particularly redundant; it introduces a completely isolated binary predicate $S(x,y)$ that does not interact with any other rule in the system.

#### (ii) Which ones increase complexity the most?
**Justification:** **Clause 3** increases computational complexity the most. 
* **Syntactic Complexity:** It requires pushing a negation through a conjunction of universal implications, triggering De Morgan's laws across quantifiers.
* **Standardization & Skolemization Complexity:** It forces the creation of multiple existential variables that must be carefully standardized ($x_1, x_2$) and mapped to distinct, arity-1 Skolem functions ($f_1(w), f_2(y)$). 
* **Combinatorial Explosion:** Distributing the resulting disjunction over the conjunctions spawns four separate clauses (C3.1 - C3.4), significantly bloating the branching factor of any automated theorem prover searching through this KB.

### Q3(d): Impact of Removing Clause 6

**Suppose clause 6 is removed. Does this impact the provability of G? Justify.**

**Justification:** Removing Clause 6 has **absolutely no impact** on the provability of G. 
Clause 6 ($\exists x. (Q(x) \wedge \neg U(x))$) generates two unit clauses: $Q(d)$ and $\neg U(d)$. As established in the refutation trace, $Q$ functions strictly as a pure positive literal, and $U$ functions strictly as a pure negative literal relative to the rest of the KB. Because they lack complementary opposites ($\neg Q$ and $U$, respectively, are missing after pruning C4 and C5 via pure $\neg R$), these unit clauses cannot resolve against any other formula to help deduce $P$. Since Clause 6 is logically detached from the core inference path, its removal leaves the unprovability of G unchanged.
